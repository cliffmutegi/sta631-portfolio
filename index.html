Response to Lecturer Feedback

Below is a transparent, point-by-point response to every concern raised in the project feedback.

1. Binary outcome felt “somewhat blunt”

Addressed by: Added new subsection 2.1.1 with clear justification (small “D_or_lower” cell size) + multinomial logistic sensitivity analysis showing consistent predictor rankings.

Impact: Demonstrates awareness of trade-offs between interpretability and granularity.

2. Limited depth on model selection & justification

Addressed by: Created dedicated section 3.3 “Model Selection Rationale” explaining why logistic, lasso, LDA, and QDA were deliberately chosen and how they complement each other.

Impact: Shows theoretical grounding and deliberate design.

3. Limited deeper checking (LDA/QDA assumptions, robust validation)

Addressed by: Added subsection 4.3.1 with Box’s M test (p < 0.001), Q-Q plots of discriminants, repeated 5-fold CV (5 repeats, <1.2% variation), and calibration curves.

Impact: Moves beyond basic metrics to proper assumption checking and stability analysis.

4. Lasso justification was thin

Addressed by: Expanded discussion to emphasize stability across CV folds (>90% retention), automatic shrinkage of correlated survey items, and superior policy interpretability for stakeholders.

Impact: Positions Lasso as the principled choice, not just the slightly better one.
